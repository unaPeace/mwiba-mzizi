This is an idea by Howard Richman, the owner of Green Elephant backpacker in Cape Town.

Despite diligently investing in insurance coverage to protect his business against unforeseen interruptions, Howard found himself disillusioned when his insurance company callously rejected his claim for business interruption due to COVID-19.

This refusal significantly impacted his business. Numerous other businesses encountered similar challenges, with insurance companies declining to fulfill claims related to COVID-19 interruptions.

While some businesses resorted to legal action against their providers and emerged victorious, many others remain burdened with unpaid claims.

Case Study

Howard needed an outlet for the stress endured due to his former insurance providers failing him. He visioned an app that would provide a space for users to share their stories about negative insurance experiences, exposing the dishonest marketing used by insurance providers.

The website could also help users better understand insurance policies and navigate the claims process empowering users to make informed decisions and advocate for themselves when necessary. So he hired me to help him develop his idea.

The prototype constructed for the client's review.

A single-page application was built with Angular. Navigation between pages is facilitated by the routing module. A service component handles retrieving and managing data with Supabase as the backend.

Angular's functionalities are utilized throughout the code. The ngIf and ngFor directive dynamically render content based on available data. The @HostListener decorator allows components to react to user scrolling. Dynamic styling adjusts based on user interaction throughout the application.

The component-based structure promotes well-organized and reusable code. Dependency injection simplifies data management, and routing allows smooth navigation. The website was built to give the client a better perspective on the practicality of his ideas.
